## 1. ESTABLISHED PHYSICS (Well-Known)

### 1.1 Virial Theorem

**Status:** Fully established since 1870 (Clausius)

**What's known:**
- 2T + U = 0 at equilibrium
- T = -V/2 (kinetic = half potential)
- Applications: stellar structure, galaxy dynamics, gas theory
- Used by Zwicky (1933) to infer dark matter

**Key figures:** Clausius, Maxwell, Rayleigh, Poincaré, Chandrasekhar, Zwicky

**Sources:**
- Wikipedia comprehensive coverage
- Standard astrophysics textbooks
- John Baez's "The Virial Theorem Made Easy"

### 1.2 Critical Adiabatic Index (4/3)

**Status:** Fully established since Chandrasekhar

**What's known:**
- γ < 4/3 → gravitational instability → collapse
- γ = 4/3 → marginally stable (relativistic gas, radiation)
- γ = 5/3 → stable (non-relativistic ideal gas)
- Critical for neutron star stability, supermassive star evolution

**Key figures:** Chandrasekhar, Jeans, Eddington

**Sources:**
- "The Dynamical Instability of Gaseous Masses Approaching the Schwarzschild Limit"
- Multiple papers on neutron star stability
- Astrophysics textbooks

### 1.3 Entropic Gravity

**Status:** Active research area, controversial

**What's known:**
- Gravity may emerge from entropy/information (Verlinde 2009)
- Jacobson (1995) derived Einstein equations from thermodynamics
- Bekenstein-Hawking entropy: S ∝ Area
- Holographic principle connections

**Key figures:** Verlinde, Jacobson, Padmanabhan, 't Hooft, Bekenstein, Hawking

**Sources:**
- Verlinde (2009) "On the Origin of Gravity"
- Jacobson (1995) thermodynamic derivation
- Nature Communications papers on holographic gravity

### 1.4 Black Hole Thermodynamics

**Status:** Well-established theoretical framework

**What's known:**
- Four laws analogous to thermodynamics
- Hawking radiation, Bekenstein entropy
- Area-entropy relationship
- Information paradox debates

**Key figures:** Bekenstein, Hawking, Penrose, Strominger, Vafa
