## 6. EXTENDED MATHEMATICS

### 6.1 Time Evolution

$$\frac{d\kappa}{dt} = \alpha R_{forcing} - \beta S_{constraint}$$

**Stability:** αR < βS → κ stabilizes
**Collapse:** αR > βS AND κ > 0.55 → runaway

### 6.2 Network Scaling Law

For systems with N agents:
$$R_{total} \propto N^2 \quad \text{(Metcalfe's law)}$$
$$S_{total} \propto N \quad \text{(linear scaling)}$$

Therefore:
$$\kappa(N) = \frac{N^2}{N + N^2} = \frac{N}{1 + N} \to 1 \text{ as } N \to \infty$$

**Implication:** Large systems trend toward κ = 1 (collapse) without active S-maintenance.

$$\kappa_{son} = \kappa_{baseline} + \alpha \cdot \kappa_{father} \cdot P_{present}$$

- α ≈ 0.4-0.6 (S-transfer efficiency)
- P_present = 1 if father present, 0 otherwise

**Extraction:** Those with κ > 0.40 extract from those with κ < 0.35 via R-programs.
